Application of Bispectrum and Convolutional Neural Network on Time Series Data (Part I)¶

Shanglin Zhou¶

Introduction¶

One interesting topic of time series study is the classification of time series data. Example is like when we have a bench of time series datasets collected from the earthquake censor, and we want to identify what kind of features in these dataset indicate the occurrence of earthquake. Then we need to classify which series is recorded just before earthquake and which is recorded during normal time. In this project, I aim to build a different model to push the limit of time series classification limit based on a subset of UCR time series database.

Data¶

The datasets I used is from UEA & UCR Time Series Classification Repository. It is an ongoing project to develop a comprehensive repository for research into time series classification. Among the datasets, I choose "Beef" dataset. This dataset consists of four classes of beef spectrograms, from pure beef and beef adulterated with varying degrees of offal. Because food spectrographs are used in chemometrics to classify food types, this dataset is used in food safety and quality assurance.

Spectral Analysis¶

Joseph Fourier showed that any periodic wave can be represented by a sum of simple sine waves. Also, there is a set of sine waves that, when sumed together, are equal to any given wave.

Sometimes it is difficult to capture the specific feature on time domain of the time series, but when we convert it to spectrum, those kind of feature may become obvious.

$$ f(t) = a_0 + \sum_k{a_k * sin(kwt+\rho_k)} $$

These sine waves each have a frequency and amplitude and phase position.

Fourier Transform¶

Idea: decompose a stationary time series $X_t$ into a combination of sinusoids, with random and uncorrelated coefficients.

$$ F_{X}(k) = \sum_{t=0}^{M-1}X(t)e^{-j \frac{2\pi}{M} t k} $$

for $k = 0, 1, . . . , M 1$, which represents frequency, M is total length of the series, and $j = \sqrt{-1}$.

Bispectral Analysis¶

Because most of the time series data are non-linear, it is better to convert the time series data to bi-spectrum form and salient the specific feature of different class of time series.

$$ B_{X}(k_1, k_2) = \frac{1}{M}F_{X}(k_1)F_{X}(k_2)F_{X}(k_1 + k_2) $$ where $0 \leq k2 \leq k1, k1 + k2 \leq \frac{M}{2}$.

bispec <- function(x, sm.c = 0.625){
  #
  xx     <- x - mean(x)
  n      <- length(x)
  m      <- trunc(n^sm.c)
  sx     <- rep(0,m)
  z      <- matrix(0, m, m)
  nsq    <- 0
  bs.out <- .Fortran("sq_bispec",
                     as.double(xx),
                     as.integer(n),
                     as.integer(m),
                     as.integer(m),
                     sx = as.double(sx),
                     z = as.double(z),
                     nsq = as.integer(nsq))
  return(list(sx = bs.out$sx, z = matrix(bs.out$z,m,m), nsq = bs.out$nsq, freqs = seq(0,0.5,length=m)))
}
